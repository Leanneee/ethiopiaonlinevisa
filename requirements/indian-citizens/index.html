Ethiopia Visa Requirements for Indian Citizens

The information on this page covers the Ethiopia Online visa process, requirements, and rules currently in place regarding the Ethiopia visa for Indian citizens. It is directed specifically to travelers using an Indian passport, unless otherwise stated.

All visitors traveling to Ethiopia are required to obtain a visa in order to gain rights of entry, except for citizens of Kenya and Djibouti, who have visa-exemption arrangements with Ethiopia, meaning their citizens don’t need to apply for a visa for a period of up to 1 year (Kenyans) and up to 3 months (Djiboutians).

Instead of having to wait in long queues at the Addis Ababa Bole International Airport to get a visa on arrival, Indian citizens can apply for and obtain an eVisa for Ethiopia online.

Apply for an Ethiopia eVisa

*Note: An eVisa is an electronic travel authorization allowing visitors to enter a specific country for a certain amount of time. The process of applying for an eVisa is much less complex than applying for a traditional visa via an embassy and is simply a case of applying online from wherever you are in the world.

Obtaining an Ethiopia tourist visa for Indian citizens is simple when completed online using the eVisa for Ethiopia system.

Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Ethiopia?

Yes, Indian citizens must have a valid visa in order to enter Ethiopia.

Although, holders of service or diplomatic passports of any country, except for Somalia and Pakistan, can enter Ethiopia visa-free for a period of up to 3 months.

India and Ethiopia have an old history of overall strong relations. However, in recent years, the economic dimension has become its most important, particularly in areas of investment, agriculture, trade, and infrastructure.

Nevertheless, prior to your visa application, you must first hold a valid passport of at least 6 months, counting from the day of your planned arrival in Ethiopia.

It’s also important to note that your single-entry visa will expire once you leave Ethiopia. Those who wish to visit Ethiopia once more should re-apply for an eVisa.

The Main Immigration and Nationality Affairs Office in Addis Ababa deals with visa extensions during visitors’ stays in Ethiopia. However, visa holders must apply for a visa extension at least a week prior to the current visa expiry date. You can apply online for a new eVisa while still in Ethiopia, as well. Overstaying your visa can lead to fines or even imprisonment.

Multiple entry visas are possible for tourists who wish to travel and enter Ethiopia more than once, although the eVisa system is unable to grant travelers multiple-entry visas. Hence, travelers should apply at their nearest Ethiopian embassy upon appointment.

No transit visa is required for Indian citizens transiting through Ethiopia without leaving the airport between flights.

How Long Does It Take to Get a Visa for Ethiopia from India?

Once the application has been submitted correctly, it usually takes 1-3 business days (24-72 hours) to process an Ethiopian eVisa. You can keep track of your visa processing procedure online.

On rare occasions, the process may take longer. The most likely cause of this will be application errors or inaccuracies. To avoid application delays, ensure that you double.check all information provided prior to submitting your Ethiopia eVisa application form.

It is advisable to apply at least 7 days prior to your trip in order to allow plenty of time for processing and/or required improvements.

When applying for an Ethiopian visa from India, the response will be emailed to the email address given in the application form. You will also get a description of conditions that apply to the eVisa. In order to gain entry to Ethiopia, a printed copy of the eVisa should be presented at the border upon arrival.

Ethiopia visa requirements for Indian citizens are listed below.

Documents Required to Obtain the Ethiopian Visa for Indian Citizens

The complete list of documents for an Ethiopian tourist visa for Indian citizens includes:

  • Passport with at least 6 months of validity from the date you intend to enter the country
  • A scanned copy of your passport bio page
  • A recent passport size digital photo (in color)
  • All details inputted corrected in the application form
  • Valid card payment (credit card or debit card)

*Note: Residents of India must also provide a valid residence permit in India.

*Note: Indian Emergency Travel Documents (ETDs) are also accepted in Ethiopia.

If you’re unsure about vaccines or other health risks before visiting Ethiopia, consult your country’s latest health advice at least 8 weeks prior to your intended trip.

eVisa application for Indian Citizens

Ethiopian Visa Application for Indian Citizens

The Ethiopian visa application for Indian citizens covers the following sections:

  • Personal information, such as full name, address, date of birth among others
  • Passport details, such as passport number, expiry date, nationality, among others
  • Your detailed travel plans, such as dates, hotel address
  • Health information such as your medical history and vaccinations, among others

Upon arrival in Ethiopia, present a printed copy of the Ethiopian eVisa at the port of entry. In this case, Addis Ababa Bole International Airport, as it is the only port of entry for eVisas for the time being.

Applicants holding dual citizenship must use the same passport to enter Ethiopia as the one used when completing the application form for the electronic visa.

*Note: Your eVisa validity starts from the day you enter Ethiopia, not the date of issue. You provide your passport to Ethiopian Customs for the purpose of validating your eVisa.
